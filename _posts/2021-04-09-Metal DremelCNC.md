Recently I've been working on my CNC. It's a copy of [DremelCNC](https://indystry.cc/dremel-cnc/), a project that uses 3D printed parts to produce quite a good machine from what I saw in videos.

Although, my experience wasn't that great. I found out that the orientation of the 3d print parts is quite critical as are also the tolerances of the holes. For example, I broke like 3 Z-Axis trying to install the linear bearing because the holes were too small. And don't get me wrong, I'm sure I was at fault for those errors. Besides, the feat of engineering of producing a CNC router with almost all the structure being 3D print, is an astonishing feat of engineering that demonstrate what a 3D printer can do.

BUT, I wanted something easier to build, that didn't have so many variables to reproduce and had the same handy form factor and be a lot more sturdy and don't degrade with time, like a PLA plastic is going to do. So enter my version of it:

![MetalDremelCNC_iso](https://user-images.githubusercontent.com/3214883/114604406-20411e80-9c99-11eb-9ec0-576c39689051.JPG){: .center-block :}

It uses the same 2020 extrusion frame, same lead-screw and linear rails that DremelCNC, but the main structures are 5mm thick steel plate. Or at least that's the theory.

It's definitely a Work In Progress, but I think it is mature enough to show to the world. My idea is to release the files as an open-source project and maybe sell plate kits in Tindie. The licensing for the project; I have to look into it. It is a remix on the dremelCNC, but I don't use any of the 3d printed parts of the original, all designed by me, so I don't know how that works

Next steps:

- Prototype it in 5mm acrylic, to check that every thing works. Right now I'm searching for a laser cutter provider that won't cost an arm and a leg. From what I have seen, my local companies, for cutting this in a CO2 laser cutter is around 150€(!!!!) Is not a problem of material, store near my house sells 500mmx500mm 5mm thick acrylic for 24€ so... is I think is basically a rip-off.

- Start searching for a machine shop and do some cost estimation for the set of pieces. The cuts shouldn't vary that much, so the price I would get for the final piece should be similar.
