Old school is cool, even more if its old school electronic tricks! 

Today at work I learned a cool use for relays. Is quite simple: A control-based relay that don't autostart in case the power supply goes down and someone rearms the braker. 
And why is useful? Imagine a complex machine or a prototype, that need a somewhat complex start procedure. You don't want to brake it if power goes off while testing.
Or what about a faulty socket, that you don't know if works correctly but cannot stay in front of a multimeter for fucks know what time.

i've only tried with a DC relay but I don't see why shouldn't work for an AC relay.
