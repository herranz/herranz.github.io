Another post about another language. This one will be in English.

Recently I applied to a CERN job offer and got rejected. There's no surprise there, was a long shot anyway. But reflecting in my CV, in what could have learned/done I started to see that in the actual
political climate, with Trump in office and Brexit already a reality, English is not anymore one of the primary languages, at least on paper, in the EU. And while globally Spanish and English are important,
I think inside the EU, specially in technical/engineering circles, their weight is diminishing in the case of English and in the case of Spanish, well don't get me started on the state of R&D and science in general in Spain.

Then, French and German are the two most important languages in the EU and their respective countries the most powerful economical, so no surprise there. Which one could I learn minimizing time and maximizing results?
Well, speaking Spanish and Catalan the answer is obvious: French.

(And don't get me wrong, I will continue studying Japanese (as to this date I'm at mid A1 level) but this will be a longer term goal.)

A few months back I read a very [interesting article](https://runwes.com/2020/02/11/howilearnedfrench.html) about how a person learned French in a year being a monolingual native English speaker. Wow!

So, being a native speaker of two languages that belong to the romance languages tree and are in proximity... Could I do the same in more or less the same time frame and get a DELF B2?

The only way to find out is trying.

*To be completely honest, I did 3 years of french while in high school but the learning method is horrendous and I don't remember almost nothing about those years.*

## My battle-plan

As engineering as taught me, starting from nothing is the least efficient way to do something. Nobody lives in a vacuum so Wesley's plan described in the article linked above is a good starting point and I can always modify the plan as I go along to adapt it to my quirks and besides " no battle plan survives contact with the enemy".

- Duolingo APP: At least half-hour every day. Good first approach to get my feet wet with the language. I will stick exclusively to this at least for the first month. Desktop version.

- French music/podcast: I will not understand anything but is free and you can check if you begin to understand words while improving the vocabulary. Spotify for the win.
Also, [The French Experiment](https://www.thefrenchexperiment.com/), [TheFrenchpod101](https://www.frenchpod101.com/) or the [InnerFrench podcast](https://innerfrench.com/podcast/) could be a good exercise specially the first to start listening to children' books.


I will be updating my progress with subsequent post on this blog.
